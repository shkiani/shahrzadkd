About me,

My name is Shahrzad and I am just finishing the first year of my PhD program in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, at the University of Toronto (UofT). My supervisor in both PhD and MASc. programs is Professor Stark C. Draper. My PhD research builds upon my UofT masterâ€™s thesis in the field of cloud and parallel high-performance computing (HPC). As part of my graduate studies, I completed related theoretical courses and was trained in the 2019 international HPC summer school, in Kobe, Japan (granted with full scholarship). My research interest is in distributed computing, approximation algorithms, coding theory and statistical learning.

Before coming to UofT, I received my B.Sc. degree in Electrical Engineering and a minor degree in Economics from the Sharif University of Technology (SUT). During my B.Sc., I conducted research on biomedical and sparse signal processing in the Image and Multimedia Processing laboratory at SUT and also spent a summer internship in the Department of Information Engineering at the Chinese University of Hong Kong in the field of network information theory.

I won gold medal in 29th Iranian National Mathematical Olympiad and am a recipient of Ontario Graduate Scholarship (OGS) for two consecutive years 2019-2020 and 2020-2021.
